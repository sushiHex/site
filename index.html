About

For 25 years I've built the systems behind believable digital characters: facial work on the Matrix Universal Capture system; hero-character cloth, hair, and skin across eight DreamWorks features; Rigging & Simulation Lead on Blizzard cinematics, covering full character and facial rigging plus hair & cloth; and real-time avatar systems at Meta (Avatars & Horizon).

That craft is now a bridge. I bring the same physics intuition and obsession with quality to applied AI: agent systems, retrieval pipelines, and the self-improving evaluation harnesses that keep them honest. Shipping simulation for film and games taught me what quality looks like frame by frame, and that's the bar I hold generative systems to.

Applied AI

Agent Systems & Evaluation

My specialty: self-improving evaluation harnesses for agentic AI. A 15-question eval matrix drove 200+ improvement iterations on a 50+-user production system, with orchestration patterns that keep autonomous agents measurable, verifiable, and honest.

Open Source

mainframe-mcp, a GPU-accelerated MCP + RAG knowledge server: Qwen3 embeddings, native reranking, hybrid search, ambient memory. Its in-repo eval harness measured +26% / +33% retrieval gains.

Oracle Agent SDK

An SDK for orchestrated deep-research agents: query classification, parallelized investigation, and convergence on high-confidence answers. Open-source release in progress.

Generative & Multimodal

Diffusion LoRA fine-tuning and evaluation (FLUX) with structured quality rubrics, including a FLUX.2 generative-typography pipeline that turns text descriptions into glyph atlases and installable fonts, scored against held-out test sets · voice-first multimodal pipelines built on modern speech synthesis.

Much of my applied-AI engineering lives behind NDAs and private repos: agentic pipelines for production character teams and physics-ML training data at Meta, plus several production-grade private systems. I'm glad to walk through it in depth.
